Frequently Asked Questions

What is UNICEF?

UNICEF (United Nations Children's Fund) is a United Nations agency responsible for providing humanitarian and developmental aid to children around the world. We work in over 190 countries and territories to protect the rights and improve the lives of every child, everywhere.

What does UNICEF do?

UNICEF focuses on child protection, education, health, nutrition, water and sanitation, and emergency relief. We advocate for children's rights and support programs that provide long-term support and immediate aid.

How can I donate to UNICEF?

You can donate online through our secure website, by phone, mail, or through monthly giving programs.

Can I sponsor a specific child through UNICEF?

Unlike traditional child sponsorship, UNICEF works at the community and national levels to improve conditions for all children. Your support helps ensure access to essential services like healthcare, education, and clean water.

How can I volunteer for UNICEF?

Volunteer opportunities vary by country. You can support us through advocacy, fundraising, or by joining local UNICEF National Committees. Visit our [Get Involved] section to learn more.
